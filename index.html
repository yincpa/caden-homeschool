World History A & B
IEM Schools · Ocean Grove Charter School · 5.0 credits
Course Overview

Covers Modern World History following California Content Standards. Topics include the Renaissance, Reformation, American and French Revolutions, World Wars I and II, the Cold War, and the world since 1945. Students study world geography including latitude, longitude, time zones, and topographical maps.

Grading
50%Chapter Assignments
50%Chapter Reviews
Textbook

Pearson AGS World History Textbook and Student Workbook (available from Wieser Educational)

Semester A — Units
Unit 3: Early Modern Times (1348–1800)

The Renaissance, The Reformation, The New Science Era, Beginnings of Global Age, Age of Kings. Reading: chapters 14–18, workbook pp. 59–86. Chapter Reviews: Ch.14 p.348, Ch.15 p.372, Ch.16 p.394, Ch.17 p.424, Ch.18 p.454.

Unit 4: Enlightenment and Revolution (1687–1850)

Age of Reason, Revolutions and Napoleon, Industrial Revolution, Revolutions in Europe and Latin America. Reading: chapters 19–22, workbook pp. 87–104.

Unit 5: A New Global Age (1840–1914)

Nationalism and Imperialism. Reading: chapters 23–24, workbook pp. 105–110.

Semester B — Units
Unit 6: World Wars and Revolutions (1905–1945)

WWI, Russian Revolution, Nationalism, Totalitarianism, WWII and Aftermath. Reading: chapters 25–27, workbook pp. 111–129.

Unit 7: The World Since 1945 (1945–Present)

Cold War, New Nations, Developing World, The World Today. Reading: chapters 28–31, workbook pp. 130–147.

Assignment Types (each unit)

Short answer · Vocabulary · Matching activities · Fill-in-the-blank sentences & timelines · True/False Reading Checks · Written definitions of Key Terms · Chapter Review at end of each chapter

a-g US History (CBi)

Course Overview

Explores major events, personalities, and social, cultural, political, economic, and technological changes shaping US history — from the Colonial Era through contemporary America, with emphasis on the 20th and 21st centuries.

Grading — Semester A
60%Daily Work
40%Key Assignments
Grading — Semester B
50%Daily Work
40%Key Assignments
10%Final Exam or Project
Textbook

United States History: Modern Times (McGraw Hill ©2023) · ISBN 9781264949557 · Digital resources provided by instructor

Semester A Units
Unit 1: A New Nation (3 weeks)

Colonial Era, American Revolution, Constitution, Civil War & Reconstruction

Unit 2: Industrialization (4 weeks)

Western Expansion, Immigration, Urbanization, Reform Movements

Unit 3: Rise of the US as a World Power & WWI (4 weeks)

American Imperialism, Spanish-American War, Panama Canal, WWI Home Front

Unit 4: Progressive Era & the 1920s (3 weeks)

Women's Suffrage, Harlem Renaissance, Prohibition, Mass Production

Unit 5: The 1930s & the New Deal (4 weeks)

Great Depression, Dust Bowl, FDR, New Deal Programs

Semester B Units
Unit 6: World War II (3 weeks)

Pearl Harbor, Allied Strategy, Home Front, Holocaust, Atomic Bomb

Unit 7: Cold War Foreign Policy (3 weeks)

Truman Doctrine, Korean & Vietnam Wars, Cuban Missile Crisis, Soviet Collapse

Unit 8: Domestic Issues After WWII (4 weeks)

Post-War Society, Presidents Truman–Johnson, Great Society, Space Race

Unit 9: Movements for Civil Rights (4 weeks)

Civil Rights Movement, MLK, Women's, Latino, Disability, LGBTQ Rights

Unit 10: Contemporary United States (4 weeks)

Watergate, Reagan, 9/11, Great Recession, Obama, Trump

Essay · Timeline · Multimedia Presentation · Political Cartoons · Document-Based Questions · Student-created project with ES approval

Final (Semester B)

Choose: Final Exam (multiple choice, short answer, essay — 3"×5" notecard allowed) OR Final Project (21st Century Issue essay/presentation, Newspaper Topics, or Decade Interview Project)

Personal Finance 1A/1B
Yolanda Hedtke · IEM Schools · 5.0 credits · Course #2E0011
Course Description

A year-long course covering all essential personal finance topics necessary to become a financially capable student. By the end, students will have a thorough understanding of personal finance and be prepared for financial responsibilities after graduation.

Grading

Daily exit tickets and end-of-unit tests. See Schoology for full grading details.

Materials

All materials provided during lessons and/or located in Schoology. No purchase required.

Units
Unit 1: Behavioral Economics

How cognitive biases and outside influences affect financial decisions

Unit 2: Banking

Bank accounts, why and how to save

Unit 3: Investing

Stock market, investment strategies, and retirement planning

Unit 4: Types of Credit

Credit, credit cards, and loans

Unit 5: Managing Credit

Credit score management and debt

Unit 6: Paying for College

Scholarships, grants, student loans, and repayment

Unit 7: Career

Job search, cover letters, resumes, interviews, and succeeding at work

Unit 8: Insurance

Types of insurance and how they work

Unit 9: Taxes

US tax system and filing a tax return

Unit 10: Budgeting

Building budgets for all areas of life

Unit 11: Consumer Skills

Savvy consumer skills, avoiding scams, fraud, and identity theft

Community Service
Young Men's Service League (YMSL) · Parent/Guardian supervised · 2.5 credits/semester · 5.0 credits full year
Course Overview

Awards academic credit for structured community service completed through the Young Men's Service League (YMSL), a nationally recognized nonprofit where mothers and sons volunteer together to support philanthropic organizations. Integrates authentic service, written reflection, and essential life skills into a year-long learning experience.

About YMSL

YMSL cultivates a spirit of giving back in young men by connecting them with meaningful service opportunities. Students engage with more than 30 philanthropies and develop real-world capabilities alongside a community of peers.

Heartful Life Management (HELM)
Claire Priddy · Ocean Grove Charter School · 2.5 credits/semester · Code: 2E7481/2E7482
Course Description

HELM develops strong social and emotional skills alongside academic excellence. Teaches practical skills to reduce stress, anxiety, and loneliness in a heart-based, nurturing environment. Covers self-development, happiness, prioritization, positivity, sleep, gratitude, embracing diversity, and coexisting with others.

Grading (each semester)
5%Discussion Thread
25%Journaling
45%Workbooks (Pear Deck)
25%Service Project
Semester 1 Units
Unit 1: Introduction to Heartfulness and Relaxation

What is Heartful Silence? Why the heart? How to practice and integrate into daily routine.

Unit 2: Deepening into Your Heart

Focus during Heartful Silence, time management, and transmission.

Unit 3: Cleaning — Feeling Lighter

Managing thoughts and disturbances, introduction to cleaning technique.

Unit 4: Self-Observation

PEMS method (Physical, Emotional, Mental, Self-development), journal writing, gratitude.

Unit 5: Tuning Into Your Heart

Decision making — emotional vs. logical, breathing with awareness.

Unit 6: Happiness Quotient

What brings happiness? Happiness and beauty in simplicity.

Unit 7: Recap + Service Project

Review of all techniques. Service project due last day of semester.

Semester 2 Units
Unit 1: Introduction to Heartfulness (Review)
Unit 2: Importance of Prioritizing

Keeping priorities straight, beating procrastination, finding balance.

Unit 3: Becoming Settled in Your SELF

Self-confidence, inner beauty, identity.

Unit 4: Fostering Positivity

Power of positivity, conflict resolution, spreading positivity.

Unit 5: Co-existing with Different Personalities

Personality types, embracing differences, handling criticism.

Unit 6: Sleeping and Resting Well

Importance of sleep for teens, how relaxation helps.

Unit 7: Attitude and Values for Excellence

Attitude for excellence, positive attitude and Heartful Silence.

Unit 8: The Art of Silence + Service Project

Self-observation, introspection. Service project due last day of school.

Service Project

Each semester ends with a community service project connecting class learning to the community. Examples: stress reduction toolkit, relaxation playlist, teaching guided meditation, art party. Includes a 2–3 paragraph reflection essay.
